Introduction

Porsche is a German automobile manufacturer specializing in high-performance sports cars, SUVs and sedans.


Origin

Ferdinand Porsche founded the company called "Dr. Ing. h. c. F. Porsche GmbH" with Adolf Rosenberger and Anton Piëch in 1931.
The main offices was at Kronenstraße 24 in the centre of Stuttgart. Initially, the company offered motor vehicle development work and consulting,
but did not build any cars under its own name. One of the first assignments the new company received was from the German government to design
a car for the people; that is, a Volkswagen. This resulted in the Volkswagen Beetle, one of the most successful car designs of all time.
The Porsche 64 was developed in 1939 using many components from the Beetle.

Reputation

In a survey conducted by the Luxury Institute in New York, Porsche was awarded the title of "the most prestigious automobile brand".
Five hundred households with a gross annual income of at least $200,000 and a net worth of at least $720,000 participated.


Reliability

A Canadian study in 2011 revealed that 97.4 percent of Porsches from the last 25 years are still on the road. In 2014, the Cayman and Boxster made the Consumer Reports list for most reliable vehicles on the road.
